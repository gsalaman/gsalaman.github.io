Thanksgiving Report

Sunday, November 27, 2011

 

Went up to Fraser after the big meal with my parents on thursday.  Looked at the report between WP and copper...copper had more stuff open.  Max decided he didn’t want to ski, and Mom was “iffy”, so they stayed back, whilst I drove down to copper.  Did the pass rather than 9 through Kremmling; takes about 75 min with clear roads and limited traffic.  They opened Windsong under the timberline, so I could bomb that run.  Checked Phresheez, thought the speed must be a mistake, so bombed it again.  Here are the charts:

They didn’t have anything new open at the top...and they marked “closed” the tree areas through to mine dump (but I still did that a couple times anyways.


Saturday and Sunday were WP days.  Got a little snow friday night...not much to speak of.  After last season, the open runs were a little bit of a disappointment; I went back to do a compare of the last few seasons to see how bad it actually was...and it turns out this year is actually just average (not bad...)  The breakdown:


This season, they had Cranmer, Upper Olympia, and Lower Hughes (Arrow) open.


Last season (2010/2011), they had everything *but* the pano and eagle running over thanksgiving.  They opened the top and eagle wind on Dec 19.


In 2009/2010, they only had the upper olympia and Cranmer open for thanksgiving.  They opened prospector (Jack and Butch) on Dec 5, and the Jane run, hilo, and all of oly on Dec 12.  The top, vasquez ridge, and the rest of Jane waited until new years, with Eagle Wind opening on Jan 6.


In 2008/2009, they only had Lower Hughes, Sale, Gemini, and the Endeavor stuff open for thanksgiving.  They opened Cranmer, Oly, Hilo, and Prospector (Butch & Jack) on Dec 7, with Jane, the C-lift, and sunnyside on dec 20.  the rest of the stuff opened up (top & eagle) by new years.


So, looking at all that, we’re in a “middlin’” year.


They did end up with some bumps on the side of Cranmer, decent, as long as you dodged the rocks in the troughs.  Saturday was crazy busy...and Cranmer was downright dangerous, between the traffic and folks screaming down (including the race teams!!!)  We bailed early...Sunday was much better...wide open.  Here are the charts:
