So now that this is workable, how do I transform Google Maps results into a model of Winter Park?


First off, how big is it gonna be?  Ideally, I see this as a coffee table replacement for the living room.  You can get base-plates that are 32x32, but you can also get plates that are 16x16.  During the prototype phase, I experimented around...you could even do smaller granularity (6x8s, for example) that would let you do different levels.  This added an additional level of complexity (how do you support each plate?) for not saving too many legos.  In the end, I went with 16x16 plates, being raised by an outside ring of 2x8 and 2x6 bricks.  Put 4 of those on a base plate, and you’ve got a “unit”.  :)


So, looking at the map, you can get something kinda rectangular by putting the Winter Park base at one corner, stretching over to Pioneer for another corner, and getting the top of Pano at a third.  This puts the fourth one a little out, but it’s workable...gives ya a grid of 8x11 16-by-16 plates, or 4 base-plates by 5 base-plates with one line of 16-by-16s.  Each base plate is about 10”, so this means something a little longer than 3’ on one side by a little more than 4.5’ on the other.  Still workable as “coffee table”.  Note though...if you add in the cirque, you get an “L” shape...and it becomes really too wide to put in the living room.  Maybe next time...


To make the big plan, I stitched together 2 screen-grabs from Google maps to get the big rectangle...and did this both for topo and with satellite.  Dropped these into visio, and put ‘em on top of each other.  Scaled them up until I had 10 squares for the 1000’ marker line, and then dropped the grid for the 16x16s on top.


Next, I printed out each 2x2 block, one sheet for topo, and one for trails.  I took the trail sheet, and colored in the white vs green, and used that to make each 16x16 template.   Then, on the topo sheet, I pencilled in every 3rd line to get the brick levels (starting with the lowest point on that 16x16).
