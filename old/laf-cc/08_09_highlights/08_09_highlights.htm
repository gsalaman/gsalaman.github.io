With the new Cabriolet lift, Winter Park *really* messed up their parking.  Last year, if you parked in the Vintage lot, you got on the bus, and it dropped ya right by the ticket window.  This year:  you get on the Cabriolet, and have to walk through their new village (hmmmm....wonder why?), past the lifts to get to the middle.  Alli's favorite quote:  "If you brought your girlfriend here to teach her to ski, she'd be done before you hit the lift".  After xmas, they started providing little wagons, but they didn't do a good job making sure they were available.  They also ran a kids shuttle; we took advantage of that a couple time.  But, the first parking choice was still  Jane...but that fills up fast, and doesn't really work if you either have beginners or someone needing lessons.  So, we found the old-town lots.  Our fav:  the Bus Barn.  You'd be first on the bus, and we could get parking there as late as 10:30ish on busy days.  Bonus:  the bus drops where the OLD busses dropped ya.  These are from a particularly rowdy ride (rowdy crowd right in front of us)...we were having a lot of fun!!!

Max did the Minnows class this year.  Each session was 3 Saturdays with the same group of kids and the same instructor.  This year, he started on level 4 with Tony, with a group of kids that, well, see for yourself:

Tony said it was easy to keep the class together.  :)  After the first 3 weeks, pretty much the whole class moved up to Level 5 (starting with poles), and Tony stayed their instructor.  At the end of the session, Tony had 'em doing Wildwood Glade, Engledive, and Hughes.

This was my best year yet for lessons.  I did two of the "Bump Jams"...a full day of intense bumps.  You start out at the top of the Jane center, and you put yourself in Level 1 (blue bumps), Level 2 (Blue-Black, can link 10-15 turns), or Level 3 (Black bumps, stop once or twice on a run).  They then further break ya up into groups.  Session 1 was with "Parti Pam", and it was a class size of 4.  That rocked!  Got a lot of great tips (backwards skiing, balance, Functional Ankle Tension, how to ditch speed, etc).  After doing that, I ended up taking Bob and Simon down Boiler:
