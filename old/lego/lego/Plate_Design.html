Now that I had a color guide and a topo guide, I used Visio to make a build plan.  Note that not all legos are available...there isn’t a 1x3 green plate, for example.  So, I made a design template based on availability, and used that to “build” up each 16x16 plate.

Now that I’ve got a 16x16 with just color, it’s time to build it up.  To do so, I started dropping the bricks down at the first brick line.  I can look at then next plate line up to get a feel of where these bricks are gonna get colored...if there’s a plate above ‘em, color doesn’t matter.  Note that the bigger blocks, while more expensive, are actually more cost-effective if you look at it as a $ per knob per “height”.  I then fill in the rest of the brick layer.

Keep this up, and you end up with a sheet of layer-by-layer instructions per 16x16 plate.

Then it’s time for the first plate layer.  Same concept as with the bricks...but I used “x”s to show where the color changes happen.   After the first plate layer and brick layer, I grey out all the blocks, and drop down the second plate layer.

Now, for the next layer, I delete all the plates, and fill in the gaps with grey blocks...then delete the bricks.  This leaves me back with the color mask, and I can cycle/rinse/repeat with the next layer
