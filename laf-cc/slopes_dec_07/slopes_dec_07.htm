December at Winter Park

The scouting report for Bob, Vanessa and Simon.

The Cheshire Cat/Jabberwocky split.  Note we've got some powder.

Looking down Jabberwocky.  Yes, this is a blue, but it's ungroomed, and has some nice, soft baby-bumps on it.  You can see Fraser Valley stretching out in the distance, and see the snowmaking on Twideele Dee.

A closer view down the slope...from those soft bumps.  You can see one of the turnouts to White Rabbit.

Riding up the Olympia lift, over White Rabbit (a groomed blue).  That cutoff we were looking at is over there on the right side.

Change of venue...this is the top of the Mary Jane mountain, right below the top of the sunnyside lift looking southwest at the top of Parsen's Bowl. 

Looking due west at Parsen's Bowl from the same spot.  There's a road that goes along the top there (very windy!) that lets you drop into the bowl wherever you want.

My first ride up the Panoramic Express.  Lots of good powder in the trees today! 

Looking straight up the lift...

The view from the top.  Very windy today, and a fair amount of wind-swept powder.  Parry's Peak is in the background, trying to peek out of a cloud. 

Here's the new trail on that ridge (Parry's Peak Trail), looking up at the top of the ridge on the top-left.  You can drop down into the trees on the right, or follow this thin groomed track back down to the lift.

Going back up the Sunnyside lift, looking west at the Bluebell trail (groomed blue).

Looking right at the bottom (Blue-Black bumpy portion) of Roundhouse.
