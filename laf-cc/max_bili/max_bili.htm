Max was a little jaundiced when we took him home.  Apparently, Jaundice is very common out here in Colorado, and is more prevalent with breast feeding and traumatic births.  

Infants are born with a certain amount of red blood cells.  These red blood cells break down into Bilirubin, which in normal folks gets processed by the liver.  Bilirubin is also broken down by UV light.  For infants, the liver typically doesn't come on line for a few days, so there's nothing to break down the Bilirubin.  Too much red blood cell breakdown, and you get too much Bilirubin, leading to the yellowish color.

There's a couple different ways of dealing with this.  If the Jaundice is mild, you can just leave your kid out in the sun.  If it's more severe, the Doc will prescribe some sort of UV treatment.  Here's a picture of Max in his little Bili-Bed:

 

The bed we got has the UV light (that blue tinge) coming from the bottom, where he sleeps on a clear, padded mattress-type pad.  It's covered by a velcro-enclosed blanket, with holes for his head and arms.  He didn't mind this *TOO* much...I guess it's a similar feeling to being swaddled, so he did okay in it.  You're supposed to leave the patient in the bed all the time, with the exception of feedings and changings.

Every morning, Max and Dad would take a trip to Avista to get a blood draw to check the Bilirubin level.  (which let Mom catch up on some rest and take a shower)  After a few days in the bed, Max's level got back to normal.  Woo hoo!
