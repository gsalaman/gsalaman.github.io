I started with prototyping Vasquez Ridge, for a couple reasons.  First, it had a well-defined, visible, well-known (at least to me) trail pattern.  Second, it was one of my favorite areas.


There are two main types of legos that I used:   plates and bricks.  The plates are the flat ones...bricks stand up three times as tall as the plates.  Did some math on the knobs; it takes 3 plates stacked on top of each other to make a bricks-worth of height.  Also, 2 knobs worth of length is equivalent to 5 plates of height, or each plate of height is 0.4 knobs.


Google Maps was my main topo generator.  They do major topo lines every 200 feet...and minor lines every 40 feet.  The minimum horizontal zoom is 1000’ in order to still get the terrain markings...but you can do 2000’ (or larger) as well.


I started with the 2000’ foot scale....looks like that would get me a workable model size (not too big).  2000’, broke up into 10 pieces, gives me 200’ per knob.  With the “knob-to-plate” ratio of 2.5/1, this means one plate is 80’ tall, or 2 topo-lines.


In doing some experimentation (with Max’s legos), it turned out that scale wasn’t giving me enough definition on the trails...it was too zoomed-out.  So, I increased the scale by a factor of 2...every minor topo line would be one plate worth of height (40’), and I broke the 1000’ into 10 squares for plates (100’, giving us that 2.5/1 ratio to make the proportions come out right).
